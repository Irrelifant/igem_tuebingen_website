Team Tübingen 2019

This year’s iGEM team of Tübingen consists of 15 highly motivated students from various study areas. It includes students of biochemistry, bioinformatics, nanoscience and molecular medicine who share the common interest to launch and manage an individual, self-organised scientific project.

Our project

The aim of the project 2019 is to generate a microbial biosensor and recycler of phosphate based on a plasmid chassis incorporating a self-kill mechanism to prevent the spread of GMOs in the environment. This also addresses the high concerns associated with GMOs and fulfills the necessary biosafety standards. The bacterium is supposed to sense and store phosphate in order to make it accessible and reusable for humanity. Since phosphate is a limited and highly sought after resource this project has environmental as well as economical relevance. Next to the development of such a GMO we also plan to model the efficiency, stability and kinetic of the self-kill mechanism.
